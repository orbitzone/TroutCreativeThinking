Rules & Conditions

1. Information on this website (including how to enter and prizes) forms part of these Rules and Conditions. Submission of an entry to The Reece Bathroom Innovation Award ("Award") is deemed acceptance of these Rules and Conditions.

2. To be eligible to enter the Award you must be:

  • an architect, interior architect, industrial designer, interior designer or other design professional who is a permanent resident of Australia;
  • or a student in an architecture, industrial design or other design discipline currently enrolled in an Australian university or institution, who is a permanent resident of Australia or the holder of a student visa permitting temporary residence in Australia valid until 30 September 2013 or beyond.

3. If the entry is being submitted by an employee, the entrant must provide written approval from their principal/employer on the entry form, otherwise the entry will be disqualified.

4. To enter, entrants must register their details on this website. Each registered entrant will receive an entry identification code. This code must be placed on the entry form and submission.

5. Entrants must complete all sections of the entry form available on this website including the declaration and approval. The completed entry form must not be attached to the submission but simply supplied with the submission; otherwise the submission will be automatically disqualified.

6. Submissions must be for a design that incorporates innovative technology in one or a range of the following products:

  • Tapware - 3 piece or mixers
  • Showers - Overhand, Hand and Rail
  • Toilets
  • Baths
  • Vanity Basins
  • Bathroom Furniture, eg Vanity Units

Entrants can choose to design their product for one of the three bathroom space categories:

  • Destination Space – Design a product that elevates the experiential value of the bathroom within the home.
  • Sustainable Space – Design a bathroom product that allows people to live more sustainably.
  • Independent Space – Design a bathroom product that enhances independent living.

7. All submissions must comprise a complete set of drawings including 2D and 3D modelling. They must be submitted online here in high resolution PDF format. They can also be submitted through the post in hard copy and on disc in high resolution PDF format. Each entry must be submitted with a brief, typed design statement of no more than 500 words. Each submission must be clearly identified with the entrant's identification code. Each page of your drawings and your design statement must be identified with your entry identification number only, if you are submitting via post your identification number must also be on the CD. To ensure anonymity, your name or any other identifying reference (eg. logos, symbols or name of practice) should not appear on the submission.

8. Each submission must be the original work of the entrant only. No group, team or collaborative entries will be accepted. The declaration of originality on the entry form must be signed by each entrant.

9. Each entrant warrants that his/her submission is entirely his/her original work and does not infringe any intellectual property rights in any other works anywhere in the world. Each entrant indemnifies Reece Pty Ltd ACN 004 097 090 ("Reece") from and against all actions, proceedings, costs, damages, claims, demands, expenses, losses and liabilities whatsoever arising from or by reason of any breach by him/her of this warranty.

All entries are to be posted to: The Reece Bathroom Innovation Award, Private Bag 109, Burwood, Victoria, 3125.

14. Each submission must be clearly identified with the entrant's identification code. To ensure anonymity, the entrant's name or any other identifying reference (eg. logos, symbols or name of practice) should not appear on the submission.

15. Student entries must be accompanied by proof of the entrant's current enrolment at an Australian tertiary institution. One of the following will be accepted as proof:

1. a standard 'Confirmation of Enrolment' letter from the tertiary institution, or 2. a letter from the tertiary institution on its letterhead confirming the entrant's status as a student, or 3. a certified copy of a valid student card.

16. Proof of enrolment should not be attached to the submission but simply supplied with the submission. Where a student is the holder of a current valid Australian student visa, a copy of the visa must also be provided. A copy of the visa must not be attached to the submission but simply supplied with the submission.

17. Submissions will not be returned. It is the responsibility of entrants to ensure they keep a copy of their submission. It is strongly advised that all entrants retain all source files should they be requested to provide them.

18. The judges' decisions will be final and no correspondence will be entered into. If an entry or entrant is found to be invalid, the judging shall be repeated. The judges will make their decisions by 30 July 2013.

19. Prizes will be awarded at the judge's absolute discretion. Student entrants are competing for one $10,000 cash prize. Professional entrants are competing for one $20,000 cash prize. There will be no obligation on the judging panel to award either the student or professional prize if they so decide.

24. All submissions will be assessed anonymously. The identity of the entrants will only be revealed once the judges have confirmed the winners. When making their assessments, the judges will have regard to the following criteria and requirements:

  • Suitability – How well does the product meet the needs of the selected category (Destination Space, Sustainable Space, Independent Space).
  • Technology – innovative design with technological attributes, new applications, enhanced capabilities of product, personalised features for user, considered how product is applicable in category space
  • Aesthetic Merit – the product needs to display design that is aesthetically focused with the inclusion of technology and functionality, showing a real understanding of its uses and prospective users. Judges will consider whether the product could add to contemporary practice in Australia
  • Functionality – design must show a real understanding of the functionality of the product.
  • Viability – Does the product have realistic potential to be manufactured using commercially available methods and materials.
  • Compliance – Must meet relevant design and legislative standards - Australian Standards, Watermark, WELS or BCA.
